Light’s revenge tour takes him to the Elven Queendom, home of his former partymate Sasha. Her reward for leaving him for dead was riches, status, and a betrothal to Sir Mikhael, second-in-command of the queendom’s elite White Knights. To “congratulate” Sasha on her engagement, Light issues her a challenge: face off against him in a giant tower shaped like a wedding cake! Although Sasha knows it’s a trap, she must rectify her former party’s mistake of leaving Light alive three years ago in order to preserve her new lifestyle. With Sir Mikhael and the White Knights in tow, she challenges Light and his crew of Level 9999 bombshells—plus a few new faces. Can they overcome the might of one of the world’s strongest nations?

Fresh from getting revenge on the elf who put an arrow in him on that fateful day, Light learns that another of his betrayers, the dark elf Sionne, is in grave danger. While conducting experiments on Gifted humans in her underground lab, she accidentally summoned a soul-eating dragon that has transformed her lab into a vast dungeon. Light now has no choice but to rescue her if he wants to get even with his old foe. So, in his guise as leader of the Black Fools, Light teams up with a party of dark elves led by Yude, a high-ranked adventurer who’s taking point on a quest to wipe this new dungeon off the map. But there’s more to Yude than meets the eye, and Light soon finds out that this potentially world-destroying dungeon isn’t the only threat he’s up against. Can Light navigate the liminal space between the worlds of the living and the dead to get revenge on Sionne before she dies some other way?

Volume 4

After crossing Sionne off his revenge list and sealing the tremor-causing interdimensional portal for good, Light returns to the Abyss, where his debrief is interrupted by news from Mei. She tells Light they’ve found his younger sister, Yume, who went missing after someone—or something—destroyed their hometown. It turns out she is still in the Human Kingdom after being rescued by Princess Lilith, who kept her on as a maid. While Light makes plans to bring Yume to the Abyss, he reminisces about his life since they last saw each other. He recounts the days after he was betrayed, going from merely surviving the Abyss with Mei to pushing past his limits, summoning more overpowered allies, and eventually transforming the world’s more notorious dungeon into his very own hidden kingdom. When Light finally meets Princess Lilith himself, they find they share a passion for improving humanity’s lot—but a dark secret plagues her kingdom.

Volume 5

While Light was busy clearing the Abyss of monsters so that he had somewhere to build his new kingdom, his former Concord of the Tribes cohort, Naano, was slowly slipping deeper into despair. Despite being showered with riches and securing a top job in the Dwarf Kingdom’s most distinguished smithy, the talented blacksmith felt the course of events was taking him further and further from his life’s goal: to craft the ultimate legendary weapon. However, an encounter with a human merchant puts him on a new, much darker path to fulfilling his dream, and with his goal now in sight, Naano will stop at nothing to craft a legendary sword, even if it means spilling the blood of countless innocents. Meanwhile, Light’s quest for revenge on Naano leads to him striking a deal with the ruler of the Dwarf Kingdom, King Dagan, but will aiding him to explore some vast, labyrinthine ruins replete with deadly traps and powerful guardians from which no one has returned prove to be an unwise undertaking?

Volume 6

The Masters hosted by the Dragonute Empire have grown increasingly concerned about their lack of knowledge surrounding the emergence of the Great Tower as well as the sudden disappearances of former members of the Concord of the Tribes, so they hatch a scheme to find out who exactly they are up against. Their plan is to get the Beastfolk Federation to declare war on the Great Tower while they watch on from afar to learn about their opponents without the need to reveal themselves. For their part, the beastfolk have their own reasons for going to war with the Great Tower. Light’s lieutenant, Ellie—aka the Wicked Witch of the Tower—has successfully conquered the elves and the dark elves, freeing the human slaves suffering under their heels in the process. Not wishing to share the same fate, the beastfolk are determined to destroy the Great Tower. But how can a relatively weak nation fight a sorceress who has her own personal army of dragons? By fighting dirty, that’s how.

Volume 7

For Light and his allies in the Great Tower, the war with the Beastfolk Federation proved to be little more than a bump in the road, but the repercussions turn out to be more of an issue than originally assumed. News of the tower’s overwhelming victory spreads around the world like wildfire, making a number of the other nations sit up and take notice, and causing the next multinational summit to be brought forward as a matter of urgency. This has the knock-on effect of accelerating the plans of Princess Lilith of the Human Kingdom, who wishes to use the event to overthrow her father and seize the crown so that she can lead her nation toward a brighter future. However, she cannot accomplish her goals alone, so she comes to Light for help. Meanwhile, the manner of the Great Tower’s victory over the Beastfolk Federation’s army attracts the attention of another cabal of powerful Masters, who are quick to send out one of their own to investigate the source of the tower’s remarkable power.

Volume 8

With the emergency summit at the Principality of the Nine fast approaching, Light must somehow find a way to raise his rank as an adventurer if he is to keep his promise to accompany Princess Lilith there as her bodyguard. Getting the Black Fools bumped up to A-rank in such a short period of time proves tricky, however, and it is only by sheer luck that a quest of sufficient prestige to merit a promotion becomes readily available. Light and the gang soon find themselves traveling by carriage to the Onifolk Archipelago as part of the retinue of the bratty Holy Princess of the onis, Yotsuha, who is returning home from the Duchy. And as chance would have it, the princess is a close personal friend of Oboro, a former member of the Concord of the Tribes. Will Light be able to get his revenge on the oni that betrayed him without jeopardizing his party’s mission to raise their rank? And what exactly has the shady, secretive Oboro been up to since the two of them last saw each other?

Volume 9

After successfully bringing another nation into their sphere of influence, Light and Princess Lilith now have all the backing they need to carry out their plot of usurping the current ruler of the Human Kingdom—Lilith’s father—and installing the princess in his place. And their timing couldn’t be more perfect, for not only are high-ranking representatives from every nation converging on the Principality of the Nine for an emergency summit, Light’s adventuring party—the Black Fools—has also been promoted to A-rank, meaning they will be in attendance as Princess Lilith’s bodyguards. Furthermore, one of the delegates attending the summit representing the Demonkin Nation just happens to be Diablo, one of Light’s former partymates that Light has sworn vengeance against for betraying him. Will Light manage to pull off the double whammy of strengthening his hand by installing someone more sympathetic to his cause on the throne of the Human Kingdom and cross a sixth name off his revenge list?

Volume 10

Despite descending into chaos due to the unexpected appearance of the Wicked Witch of the Tower, the emergency summit at the Principality of the Nine was a success in one crucial respect: The former Princess Lilith was now Queen Lilith of the Human Kingdom. But despite everything being done by the book, the change of monarch has deeply angered the Demonkin Nation, especially as it quickly became apparent that an affiliation with the Wicked Witch and the nations she had brought to heel was how Lilith secured enough votes to get what she wanted. Threats of harsh reprisals against the Human Kingdom soon follow, and with her back against the wall, Lilith turns to Light and his allies to save her nation from this new threat. Meanwhile, he sees this as the perfect opportunity to ratchet up his campaign against his sworn enemy, Diablo, who retaliates by enlisting the services of the Bourreaux, the world’s deadliest gang of assassins, led by the deeply deranged Master Gira!

Volume 11

After going toe-to-toe with Gira—the most homicidal of the Demonkin Nation’s Masters—Light is ready to refocus his efforts on Diablo, one of the eight sworn foes who betrayed him. He intends to totally destroy the demon’s aristocratic lifestyle before capturing him and imprisoning Diablo like all the rest before him, but Light hasn’t counted on the intervention of the last two Masters from the Demonkin Nation: the expert brawler Goh and the gruesome mad scientist Doc. And as if that weren’t enough, the leader of the demons—Prince Voros—appears to have a destructive secret weapon up his sleeve. A series of climactic showdowns prompts Light to finally let the strongest Level 9999 warriors in his arsenal loose on the surface world—including Aoyuki, whose superpowered monster army at last gets its chance to shine! These intense battles will be the biggest challenges Light and his gang have faced yet, but nothing can prepare him for the heartbreaking tragedy that is about to play out.

Volume 12

Light has eliminated six of his eight sworn enemies. Now the next target is Santor, the centaur from the party that left Light for dead three years ago in the Abyss. As the heir to his nation’s throne, Santor joins his people in escalating hostilities against the Wicked Witch of the Tower, but Phinea, Santor’s half sister, knows this confrontation will doom their country and the entire centaur race. Desperate to prevent war, Phinea and her friend Palu set out for the Great Tower to negotiate peace with the witch. Unfortunately, they never make it! Betrayed by one of their own, Phinea barely escapes while Palu is captured. Now alone and hunted, Phinea has no allies and no clear path forward—until she crosses paths with Elio, the young former adventurer who now lives the life of a simple farmer. Can Phinea reach the Wicked Witch before Santor’s rebellion ignites a catastrophic war? And can the centaur princess trust a human boy to help her save everyone she loves?
